# Authority outreach playbook

## Goal and approval boundary

Earn relevant mentions, referral visits, and qualified enquiries by contributing useful expertise. Research, drafting, and WebiQQ publishing can proceed internally. Sending email, submitting a listing, requesting a review, or changing a client website requires business approval.

## Qualification gate

A target must have clear GCC/business-technology relevance, an identifiable publisher, a genuine reader purpose, maintained indexed pages, and no obvious unrelated link inventory. Reject guaranteed rankings, paid dofollow links, private networks, mandatory exact-match anchors, and undisclosed sponsorship.

## Process

1. Read recent relevant coverage and record the editor, section, angle, and reader benefit.
2. Offer one tailored idea and concise outline.
3. Disclose WebiQQ's commercial interest and any client relationship.
4. Follow up once after 7–10 business days, then close unless invited to continue.
5. Link to the most useful resource using `WebiQQ`, the resource title, or its plain URL.

## Measurement

When permitted, use `utm_source=<publisher>&utm_medium=referral&utm_campaign=authority_2026&utm_content=<asset>`.

Track response, acceptance, publication URL, indexation, referral sessions, engaged sessions, contact submissions, and assisted enquiries. Judge relevance and business value, not domain metrics or link count alone.

## Monthly rhythm

- Publish or materially improve one useful reference asset.
- Send 4–6 approved personalised pitches.
- Complete one legitimate entity/directory profile.
- Review new/lost links, referral traffic, enquiries, and unindexed publications.
